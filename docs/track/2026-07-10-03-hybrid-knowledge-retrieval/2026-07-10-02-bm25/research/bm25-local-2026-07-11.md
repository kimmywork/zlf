# BM25 local functional baseline — 2026-07-11

## Method

The reproducible `zlf-index` release example builds 1K and 10K chunk-documents in one Tantivy batch, replaces 100 documents in one update batch, runs 100 deterministic exact-relevance queries at top-10, reopens the index with a fresh reader, repeats the queries, and records disk size and process peak RSS.

```bash
cargo run --release -p zlf-index --example bm25_benchmark -- 1000
cargo run --release -p zlf-index --example bm25_benchmark -- 10000
```

Machine: Apple M2 Pro, 32 GiB. Full values and frozen generous regression budgets are in `bm25-local-2026-07-11.json`.

## Summary

| Chunks | Build | Replace 100 | Warm p99 | Fresh-reader p99 | RSS | Disk | MRR / nDCG@10 / Recall@10 |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1,000 | 184.3 ms | 193.4 ms | 12 µs | 14 µs | 154.1 MB | 66.4 KB | 1.0 / 1.0 / 1.0 |
| 10,000 | 200.0 ms | 210.7 ms | 13 µs | 12 µs | 158.5 MB | 501.8 KB | 1.0 / 1.0 / 1.0 |

The faster apparent 10K build rate reflects fixed analyzer/Tantivy startup costs and is not treated as a scaling claim.

## Interpretation and limits

- This is a deterministic functional/regression fixture, not a claim about general retrieval quality.
- `fresh_reader` closes and reopens Tantivy in the same process; it validates reopen state but is not an OS page-cache cold run.
- Peak RSS includes the process, analyzer dictionary, Tantivy, and allocator, not only index data.
- EnterpriseKB/SciFact/BEIR orchestration and true cold-process measurement remain owned by Stage 06 under the accepted function-first change.
- The 100K tier is optional (`at most 100K`) and was not needed to establish the first local functional baseline.
